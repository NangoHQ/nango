# Overview

To authenticate with Salesforce (Client Credentials), you will need:

1. **Client ID** – The Consumer Key from your Salesforce Connected App or External Client App.
2. **Client Secret** – The Consumer Secret from your Salesforce Connected App or External Client App.
3. **Hostname** – Your Salesforce instance hostname (e.g. `acme.my.salesforce.com`).

This guide walks you through obtaining these values.

### Prerequisites

- A Salesforce account with administrative privileges to create Connected Apps or External Client Apps.

### Instructions

#### Step 1: Create a Connected App (or External Client App)

1. Log in to Salesforce and go to **Setup**.
2. In the Quick Find box, enter **App Manager**, then select **App Manager**.
3. Click **New Connected App** (or **New External Client App**).
4. Fill in the required basic information (Name, API Name, Contact Email).
5. Check **Enable OAuth Settings**.
6. For **Callback URL**, you can enter `https://api.nango.dev/oauth/callback` (even though it's not used for Client Credentials, it's often required by the UI).
7. Under **Selected OAuth Scopes**, add at least `Access and manage your data (api)`.
8. **Crucial:** Enable the Client Credentials flow:
   - For **Connected Apps**: Check **Enable Client Credentials Flow**.
   - For **External Client Apps**: Under **Flow Enablement**, check **Enable Authorization Code and Credentials Flow**.
9. Click **Save**.

#### Step 2: Assign an Execution User (Connected Apps only)

For Connected Apps using Client Credentials, Salesforce requires you to specify an execution user:

1. In the **App Manager**, find your app, click the dropdown arrow next to it, and select **Manage**.
2. Click **Edit Policies**.
3. Under **Client Credentials Flow**, search for and select an **Execution User**. This user's permissions will be used for the API calls.
4. Click **Save**.

#### Step 3: Obtain Client ID and Client Secret

1. In the **App Manager**, find your app and select **View** (for Connected Apps) or go to the **Settings** tab (for External Client Apps).
2. Click **Manage Consumer Details** or **Consumer Key and Secret** to view your credentials.
3. Copy the **Consumer Key** (Client ID) and **Consumer Secret** (Client Secret).

#### Step 4: Find your Hostname

Your hostname is the part of your Salesforce URL before `.my.salesforce.com` (e.g., if your URL is `https://acme.my.salesforce.com`, your hostname is `acme.my.salesforce.com`).

#### Step 5: Enter credentials in Nango

1. In the Nango Connect UI or dashboard, select the Salesforce (Client Credentials) integration.
2. Enter your **Hostname**, **Client ID**, and **Client Secret**.
3. Click **Authorize** to establish the connection.

You are now connected to Salesforce using Client Credentials.
